 > &mdash;Isaiah 60:19-21

After the confession of sin in Isaiah 59, the prophet declares a command: “Arise, shine, for your light has come… Lift up your eyes and see… then you shall see and be radiant; your heart shall thrill and exult.” 

When we lift our eyes and behold the glory of the Lord, our hearts are filled with a deep, trembling joy that comes with holy fear and reverence before His majesty. The radiance Isaiah speaks of isn’t something we produce ourselves; it is the reflection of God’s glory upon His people. This promise ultimately points to Jesus Christ, the **Light of the World (John 8:12)**. 

Through His saving work, God brings His presence back to His people and **calls us out of darkness into His marvelous light (1 Peter 2:9)**. Isaiah declares that **“the Lord will be your everlasting light” (Isaiah 60:20)** and that His people will be made righteous by **“the work of my hands, that I might be glorified” (Isaiah 60:21)**. Our hearts thrill with joy because of what Christ has done for us, yet we stand before Him with awe and reverence. Lux Aeterna is a reminder to lift our eyes to the everlasting light of Christ and live as reflections of His glory.
